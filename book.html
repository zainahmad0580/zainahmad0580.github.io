The Book You Wish Your Parents Had Read

By Philippa Perry

This book is about how we have relationships with our children, what gets in the way of a good connection and what can enhance it.

First published March 7, 2019

Summary

The most influential relationships are between parents and children. Yet for so many families, these relationships go can wrong and it may be difficult to get back on track. In The Book You Wish Your Parents Had Read (and Your Children Will Be Glad that You Did), renowned psychotherapist Philippa Perry shows how strong and loving bonds are made with your children and how such attachments give a better chance of good mental health, in childhood and beyond.

    She'll help you to:
  • Understand how your own upbringing may be impacting upon your parenting style
  • Contain, express, accept and validate your own and your child's feelings
  • Understand that all behaviour is communication
  • Break negative cycles and patterns
  • Accept that you will make mistakes and what to do about them

Almost every parent loves their children, but by following the refreshing, sage and sane advice and steps in this book you will also find yourselves liking one another too.

Reasons I enjoyed this book

Parenting Challenges

The book covers a wide range of common parenting issues, such as dealing with emotions, setting boundaries, and fostering healthy relationships. This makes it relevant to parents of children of all ages, from infants to teenagers.

Self Help

The author emphasizes the importance of empathy and understanding in parenting. She encourages parents to view their children as individuals with their own thoughts, feelings, and perspectives, rather than as extensions of themselves. This approach can help parents develop stronger, more meaningful relationships with their children.

Practical Advices

The book is written in a clear and concise language that makes it easy for readers to understand and apply the advice given. The author shares numerous anecdotes and case studies that help readers see how the advice can be applied in real-life situations.
